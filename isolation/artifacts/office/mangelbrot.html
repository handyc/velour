wang tiling

Each sprite's four edges are averaged into a colour (skipping the magenta key). For every cell of a 12 × 12 grid the picker finds the tile whose left + top edges best match the cell to the west and the cell to the north, ties broken by drawing uniformly from the top 6 candidates — generous enough that the tiling stays varied, strict enough that adjacent edges blend.

hex wang tiles

A complete 4-colour hex Wang set has 46 = 4096 tiles — one tile for every combination of edge colours. Click Generate Hex Set to build the whole spritesheet (4096 × 4096 px, shown scaled). Each tile is a hexagonal Mandelbrot crop with 4 px coloured stripes on the 6 edges. Generate Hex Tiling then lays a random hex tiling: for every cell the picker reads the colours of already-placed neighbours and indexes directly into the full set (no fuzzy matching needed, every constraint has an exact match). Each sprite picks a curated Mandelbrot center, perturbs it, and chooses a random log-span in 2-10‥20. The 256-entry palette is built as four 64-step ramps through the 4 RGB bases. Per-sprite a tiny ±6/channel drift is applied to each base, so the sheet's colour scheme walks smoothly instead of jumping.
