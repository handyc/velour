Muka — syntactic trees

Muka is the Oromoid word for "tree". The app started as a Konso showcase (Lowland East Cushitic, ~250k speakers, SW Ethiopia, canonical SOV in the typology literature) and has since expanded to African languages broadly — Afroasiatic (Semitic, Cushitic, Chadic, Berber, Omotic), Niger-Congo (Bantu, Kwa, Mande, Atlantic, Volta-Niger), Nilo-Saharan, Khoe-Kwadi/Khoisan, isolate (Hadza), Malagasy, Afrikaans, pidgins/creoles — plus a growing set of world classics (Sanskrit, Latin, Ancient Greek, Hittite, Mandarin, Japanese, and more). Trees are Chomsky-style labelled brackets; most entries are illustrative and a reader with the grammar in hand should upgrade them with a page citation.
