Upload any RGBA image. For every interior pixel the tool samples it + its 6 offset-r hex neighbours, quantises the 7-sample group into a 4-colour palette (0 = centre pixel exact, 1–3 = three neighbours furthest from centre), and records the 6-tuple of palette labels as a CA-rule key. Pixels sharing a 6-tuple are aggregated; their palettes averaged. The output is a ranked catalogue of hex-flower configurations the image actually exhibits — an identity (no-change) CA rule densely populated only where the image has corresponding texture.
