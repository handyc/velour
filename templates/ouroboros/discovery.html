What "fixed point" means here. R★'s LUT is exactly 16,384 bytes — the same as a 128×128 image with 2 bits per cell. Laying R★'s LUT out as that image and running R★ on itself for 16 ticks reproduces R★ pixel-for-pixel. By construction the chain output equals the chain input, so once you reach R★ you stay at R★. The chain has no decay path; this is why we mark #{{ seed_pk }}'s effective depth as ∞.

Why the catalogue lists both

#{{ seed_pk }} is the famous seed — found by deep-chain GA as a 13-byte mutation of #116 that, against all odds, navigates exactly into R★'s basin. It earns its place in the archive because the chain dynamics are extraordinary, not because the seed itself is a quine.

#{{ fp_pk }} is R★ itself — independently discovered by the L0 fixed-point quine search (l0-fp-class4) which targets strict SR = 1.0 rules directly. It would exist in the catalogue with or without #{{ seed_pk }}; the two finds happen to point at the same destination.

The same R★ being arrived at by two completely different methods — a 130-step metachain walk from a GA-mutated seed, and a direct fixed-point search — is a small piece of evidence that this rule has the kind of dynamical structure (large basin, strong convergence) you would expect of a "natural" attractor in K=4 hex rule-space, not an artefact of either search method.
