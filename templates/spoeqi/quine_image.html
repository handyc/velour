image → quine test

Upload any image — a painting, a photo, a drawing. It gets centre- cropped, posterised to 4 colours, resized to 128×128 (=16,384 cells), and interpreted as a K=4 hex CA's rule LUT. The same LUT is then used as the rule's own initial state and stepped 16 ticks; how much survives unchanged is the self-reproduction score (SR). Painting quines are extraordinarily rare — random 4-colour grids score around 0.25 — but every now and then a Klimt or a Miró geometry lines up just right.
