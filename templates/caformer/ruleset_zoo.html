Why the small end is worth pursuing

At the smallest rule sizes (2→1, 3→1, 4→1, 5→1), the LUT search space is small enough to enumerate exhaustively:

  • 2→1: 416 = 4.3 billion possible rules. Brute force in a few CPU-hours. We could have the COMPLETE catalogue of every 2→1 quine.
  • 3→1: 464 ≈ 3.4 × 1038 — too many to enumerate, but the Ouroboros manifold is dense enough that GA sampling will hit hundreds of class-4 quines in an hour.
  • 4→1, 5→1: still tractable; the search-space-to-quine- density ratio becomes very favourable.

Useful roles for tiny rules:

  • 2→1 as logic primitives: CA-native NAND/NOR/XOR-equivalent gates. Compose into logic families.
  • 3→1 majority votes: 3-input symmetric functions for ensemble decisions across the system.
  • 4→1 mode selectors: 256 distinct LUTs, perfect for small categorical filters (e.g. a 4-way intent router could live here instead of at 7→1).
  • Cross-scale gating: any small rule's output cell can be wired into a larger rule's input port (see cell8) to compose hierarchies of computation.

Multi-resolution storage hierarchy (the next move)

Every rung of this ladder is a valid resolution at which to store a trained chain. A chain that no longer needs full fidelity can be downsampled by powers of 4:

256×256  →  128×128  →  64×64  →  32×32  →  16×16  →  8×8  →  4×4  →  2×2
64 KB    →  16 KB    →  4 KB   →  1 KB   →  256 B  →  64 B → 16 B  →  4 B

      hot          cool          cold        dust       topic-flag
      (recently used)                                   (~1 byte of info)

Memory ageing becomes graceful resolution loss instead of binary deletion. Old knowledge persists at low resolution at almost no cost — a 2×2 chain is 4 bytes. Promotion: when a dust chain is matched by an incoming query, retraining at higher resolution uses the dust as a seed instead of starting from scratch.

The integration: the trained dispatcher decides query-by- query what resolution suffices. A quick semantic match? Hit the 16×16 chain in microseconds. Need byte-exact byte-by-byte response? Pipeline up to 128×128, paying 256× more compute for precision. Same query, multiple cost/quality levels. The multi-resolution stack is the CA-native expression of how brains do approximate-then-refine cognition.
