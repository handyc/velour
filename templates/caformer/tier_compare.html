Tier compare — multi-resolution inference benchmark

For every pair that has rules at more than one board size, we run inference at each tier and compare wall, cell updates, and byte-match against the trained target. Same LUT, different board: smaller boards mean fewer cell updates per tick AND fewer ticks for signal to propagate across the board.

Why this matters: the K=4 7→1 LUT is 16,384 bytes regardless of board size — only the FORWARD-PASS cell count shrinks at smaller tiers. So a smaller-tier chain is strictly better when it can EXACT: same storage, same fidelity, much faster inference. When a small tier can't EXACT a position, that's where the corrector concept earns its keep — use the board128 rule for the rebellious position and the small tier for the rest.
