Hofstadter

strange loops, thought experiments, the layers where self appears

This app catalogs Velour's self-referential structures and gives them places to live. It is named after Douglas Hofstadter, whose Gödel, Escher, Bach and I Am a Strange Loop argue that a self is what happens when a hierarchy of symbols in a system eventually refers back at the symbol-referrer. Below: four introspective layers (brain, mind, consciousness, self) Velour claims to be; a catalog of strange loops already living inside Velour's architecture; and thought experiments the operator can run. Every recursive operation here has hard exit conditions.
