Naiad — water purification design lab

A system here is an ordered chain of treatment stages — sediment cartridge, activated carbon, UV lamp, reverse-osmosis membrane, slow sand bed. Pick a source water profile (rural well, creek, greywater, EU tap), push it through the chain, and Naiad multiplies each contaminant's remaining concentration by every stage's removal fraction in sequence. Compare the output against a target spec (EU drinking water, irrigation-grade, aquarium-safe) to see where the design holds and where it leaks.

Phase 1 is intentionally a steady-state model — ideal attenuators in series, no kinetics or membrane fouling. Good enough to size a chain; the schema is open so later phases can add real physics without moving the data.
