{# Cellular sublab — tiles-are-population spatial GA on hex topology. #}

An 8×8 pointy-top hex tiling of CA tiles where the grid is the population. Every tile holds one genome + palette + live grid. Every few hundred ms a tournament round picks a random tile and one of its 6 hex-neighbours, scores both, and replaces the loser with a mutated copy of the winner. Watch how good rules sweep regions: palette inheritance makes the spread visible, and a green border on freshly-replaced tiles fades over five seconds so you can see the wave move.

Self-similar substrate: the hex CA inside each tile and the hex tiling of the population share the same 6-neighbour topology. Selection pressure flows through the same shape the rules operate on.

rounds: 0

Same engine as Classic and Filmstrip — K=4 hex CA, 16×16 grid, 4096-byte packed genome — used at two levels: each cell hosts a full CA, and the 64-tile grid hosts a cellular GA. Cost per round is two 25-tick fitness evals (~50 µs); at 2 rounds/sec that's a tiny fraction of CPU even with 64 tiles all stepping their own CAs in parallel.

Topology is toroidal pointy-top hex (odd rows shifted right by half a tile). The 6 neighbour offsets follow the standard "odd-r offset" convention; the same library that handles cell-level hex inside each tile would handle the population-level hex unchanged. Selection mechanic is tournament-with-mutation; future follow-ups: crossover-replace (true spatial breeding) and the "tide" simultaneous variant, both already sketched.
