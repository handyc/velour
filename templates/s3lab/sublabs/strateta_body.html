{# Strateta sublab — K=256 image-CA library. #}

A 16×16 library of K=256 hex CA rules. Upload an image and each of the 256 entries gets its own 256-colour palette sampled from a 16×16 region of the picture — together the library reads as a 256×256-pixel mosaic preview of the source. Multiple images may be uploaded; each entry randomly picks one as its source.

Two refine modes, switchable mid-evolution: edge-of-chaos hunts for 110-like dynamics regardless of palette (same fitness as Stratum and Classic); pixel-faithful scores each entry by how closely its CA output (after a few steps from a fresh random grid) matches the entry's source-image region pixel-for-pixel. The second mode trains the CA to render the image — its attractor becomes a compressed encoding of the source. Click any tile to download its genome as hexnn-genome-v1 JSON.

Engine: same hexnn_engine.mjs Stratum uses, dialled to K=256. Per-genome cost: 16,384 prototypes × (7 keys + 1 out) = 131 KB; the full library = ~33 MB browser memory. Inner ticks at 400 ms by default keep the per-frame budget comfortable on most machines (256 entries × 16×16 grid × ~64 lookups ≈ 4 M ops/tick).

Pixel-faithful fitness: each entry runs PIX_STEPS=10 from a fresh random grid, then its (palette-rendered) output is compared pixel-for-pixel against its source-image region. Score = 1 - mean squared RGB distance / 195,075. A perfect match scores 1.0; pure noise scores ~0.5; an inverted palette near 0. The mode is most interesting after image upload — without source regions, it silently falls back to edge-of-chaos.
